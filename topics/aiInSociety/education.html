The Uses of AI in Education

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is becoming more common in education, and it is used in many different ways to support both students and teachers. One of its main uses is personalized learning, where AI systems adjust lessons based on a student’s performance. Platforms like Khan Academy and Duolingo are good examples of this.

Figure 1: Khan Academy
Figure 2: Duolingo

Examples

  • An AI system gives easier practice questions when a student keeps getting wrong answers, then increases difficulty as they improve.
  • A learning platform repeats certain lessons when a student shows difficulty understanding a topic.
  • An automated system checks quizzes and provides scores instantly without teacher intervention.
  • A virtual assistant answers common student questions about lessons or assignments anytime.

How AI can be Harmful or Helpful

AAI in education can be both helpful and harmful depending on how it is used. On the positive side, it helps students learn faster by providing instant feedback and explanations.

Helpful:

  1. 1

    A student receives immediate corrections after answering a question incorrectly.

  2. 2

    An AI tutor explains a complex topic in a simpler and more understandable way.

  3. 3

    A system converts text into audio to help students who have difficulty reading.

    4. Harmful:

  4. 1

    Unequal access to AI tools creates gaps between students.

  5. 2

    Lack of original content or creativity from both the students and teachers that use AI for generating materials.

  6. 3

    The credibility of one's work in the educational sector will be in doubt.

Utilizing AI in Education

Figure 3: Traditional Learning

To properly utilize AI in education, it should be treated as a support system rather than a replacement for actual learning. AI is most effective when it helps students understand concepts, not when it does the work for them. As students, we still need to develop critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and the ability to analyze information on our own. If we rely too much on AI, we might finish tasks faster, but we won’t really learn anything in the long run.

At the same time, AI can be very useful when used correctly. It can act like a study partner that is available anytime, especially when a teacher is not around. It can break down difficult topics into simpler explanations, give practice exercises, and even help identify areas where a student is weak. However, the responsibility is still on the student to actively engage with the material instead of passively accepting answers.

Teachers also play an important role in utilizing AI effectively in education. They can guide students on how to use AI ethically and design activities that require understanding, such as asking students to explain their reasoning or apply concepts in different situations. This prevents students from simply copying answers and encourages deeper learning. Schools can also implement rules and policies to make sure AI is used fairly and responsibly.
