Risks of AI in the Future

Chris Ivan Maghirang UI Designer
April 2026
7 min read
In This Article
  • 1. Job Loss and Work Displacement
  • 2. Spread of Misinformation and Deepfakes
  • 3. Loss of Privacy and Data Misuse

Job Loss and Work Displacement

AI's potential to replace many human professions in the future, particularly those involving repetitive or predictable work, is one of its biggest risks. As AI develops, businesses may decide to replace human workers with AI systems due to their speed, affordability, and lack of exhaustion. Millions of workers who depend on these professions and who lack the skills to go into new positions could be impacted.

Figure 1: AI Replacing Humans

Why this is A Risk

If AI takes over too many jobs too quickly, the unemployment rate could rise, and many people may struggle financially. Not everyone can easily shift to new careers that require technical skills, which could widen the gap between those who understand AI and those who don’t.

Example

  • Self-checkout machines and AI cashiers might replace workers in supermarkets and retail stores.
  • AI-powered customer service bots could reduce the need for human call center agents.
  • Automated data entry systems could replace office clerks and administrative assistants.

Spread of Misinformation and Deepfakes

AI can generate extremely realistic images, videos, and even voices. In the future, these AI-generated media (especially deepfakes) could be used to spread false information or manipulate people’s opinions. This becomes dangerous when fake content looks real enough to fool the public.

Why this is a Risk

Misinformation can create confusion, damage reputations, influence elections, and cause panic. When people can’t distinguish real information from fake, trust in media and institutions drops, making society more unstable.

Figure 1: Human AI

Example

  • A deepfake video of a political leader making a fake announcement could cause chaos or influence public decisions.
  • Fake news stories produced by AI have the potential to spread rapidly on social media, leading many people to believe them before they are verified.
  • Edited photos or videos could be used to accuse a person of something they never did.

    • Loss of Privacy and Data Misuse

    Data, including images, messages, browsing history, voice recordings, and even biometric data, is crucial to AI systems. Companies and governments may utilize this information improperly in the future, either on purpose or as a result of data breaches. AI may also follow individuals without their awareness.

    Why this is a Risk

    Personal data could be used to manipulate behavior, invade privacy, or even discriminate against certain individuals. If AI knows too much about people’s habits, preferences, and locations, it becomes easier for organizations to control or influence them.

    Figure 1: Data Misuse

    Example

    AI apps might track users’ conversations or movements to target them with ads or political messages.

    Finishing Thought

    While AI has numerous advantages, there are also significant possible concerns. It has the potential to displace human labor, increasing skill gaps and unemployment. Deepfakes and misinformation produced by AI have the potential to disseminate false information that deceives people and undermines confidence. Privacy issues arise from the extensive usage of personal data, particularly when it is mishandled or disclosed. Last but not least, if AI systems are educated on faulty data, they may make unfair or biased conclusions that impact crucial domains like recruiting, loans, and law enforcement. All things considered, these dangers demonstrate that although AI is strong, it requires cautious management and moral principles to avoid harm.
