About Vince Harvey Bobiles

Greetings, I am Vince Harvey Bobiles, A 3rd year college student and a 2nd year Computer Science shifter student at the University of the East. I am currently working as a content manager for VerifAI, where I am responsible for creating and managing content especially those related to AI risks, scams, and misinformation. Currently, I am studying how I can host my own AI inside my own computer based on open source AI models and how to train it. As I am sure such a project will be a challenge, I am excited to learn and explore the possibilities of hosting and training my own AI model. I am also interested in learning about the ethical implications of AI and how to ensure that AI is used responsibly and ethically. I am looking forward to contributing to the field of AI and making a positive impact on society as a software engineer in the future.

I have a lot of things I am interested in, making it hard to focus on just one. While I am currently focused on AI, I also like to write poems, read books, and play video games. I am also interested in learning about different cultures and languages, and I hope to travel the world someday to experience them firsthand. I am a curious and open-minded person, and I am always eager to learn new things and explore new ideas. I believe that there is always something new to discover and learn, and I am excited to see where my interests and passions will take me in the future.

But above it all, I am passionate about understanding humans and their nature. You might think that as someone trying to become a software engineer, I would be more interested in technology than people and that I am misplaced, that I should study psychology, philosophy or courses related to it instead, but I dont think so. I do not necessarily have to study in a specific place since humans are everywhere, there are humans in every field, and human nature is everywhere. Studying philosophy or psychology in a college will just let me understand how the professor specifically understands human nature and how he interprets the way the people before him/her understood humans as well, but everyone has their own understanding of human nature and that is what especifically makes studying humans hard, and hard to print it out in a book. Understanding humans is not only by reading books or listening to lectures, but by diving into the world and experiencing it, by talking to people, by observing them, by trying to understand their emotions, motivations, and behaviors. And I believe that understanding human nature is crucial for creating technology that is truly useful and beneficial for people. I am also of course very interested in exploring the ethical implications of technology and how it can be used to improve people's lives. Ultimately, I believe that technology should serve humanity, and I am committed to using my skills and knowledge to create technology that is truly beneficial for people. And also, I have to earn a living (hehehe) becoming a software engineer is a good way to do that and explore the world while at it since I can work remotely, a means that I enjoy to an end which is my bigger goal.
