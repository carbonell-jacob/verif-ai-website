About Isaac Bascos

I am a second-year Computer Science student who is passionate about cybersecurity. I’ve always been interested in how systems work and how they can be protected from threats. This curiosity is what pushed me to explore technology more deeply and learn how ethical hackers think and operate. Even though I sometimes struggle with problem-solving in programming, I don’t let that stop me from improving. I see every challenge as part of my growth, and I continue to learn step by step. In the VerifAI project, my role as a Navigation Expert is very important because I am responsible for designing the structure of the website and making sure all pages are properly connected without broken links.

Outside of school, I spend most of my time experimenting with different tech concepts and improving my programming skills. I'm passionate about learning and challenging myself, even when things get difficult. I'm honest about my weaknesses—procrastination is something I actively work to overcome. In my free time, I play video games such as Mobile Legends, Valorant, and Red Dead Redemption 2. On weekends, I spend quality time with my girlfriend, which helps me balance my academic and personal life. I admit that procrastination is one of my weaknesses, but I am actively working on it by trying to complete my tasks earlier and manage my time better. My goal is to continue improving and eventually build a career in cybersecurity.

Right now, I am working on the VerifAI project as a Navigation Expert, where I focus on designing the website structure and making sure all links are properly connected without errors. This experience is helping me understand how systems are organized and how important it is to maintain a smooth user flow in a team project. Although I still struggle with programming and problem-solving, I use these challenges as motivation to improve and learn more. In the future, I want to focus on cybersecurity and develop the skills needed to protect systems and understand how hackers think. I know I still have a lot to learn, but I am willing to put in the time and effort to grow. My goal is to keep improving, stay consistent, and eventually become someone who can contribute to building secure and reliable technology.
