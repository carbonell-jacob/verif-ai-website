About Jacob Carbonell

Hi, I’m Jacob Carbonell, a second-year Computer Science student at the University of the East Manila. I chose this field because I enjoy solving problems and building things that people can actually use. I’m still learning and exploring different areas in tech, but I already know that I want to be part of something meaningful. I want to work with people who have the same goals as me, people who are motivated, consistent, and willing to grow together. For me, it’s not just about finishing projects, but also about contributing to something that has real value.

Outside of school, I spend a lot of my time coding and improving my skills, whether it’s through small projects or just experimenting with ideas. When I’m not coding, I like to play chess because it helps me think strategically and stay focused. I also take care of my fish, which gives me a break from screens and helps me relax. Music is another big part of my life, and I play both piano and guitar during my free time. Sometimes I also play video games like NBA 2K25 just to unwind. I would describe myself as a chill person, but when I have something to do, I prefer to finish it as soon as possible so I can enjoy my hobbies without worrying about unfinished work.

Right now, I’m working on a game for our software engineering course, which is helping me understand how development works in a team setting. I’ve also created a flashcard app, which is one of the projects I’m proud of because it solves a real problem for studying. In the future, I want to focus more on AI modeling and build projects that align with that path. I know there’s still a lot for me to learn, but I’m willing to put in the time and effort to get there. My goal is to keep improving, stay consistent, and eventually become someone who can create useful and impactful technology.
